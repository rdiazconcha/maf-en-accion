# Expense Report Policy - Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What expenses can I claim?
You can claim reasonable business expenses incurred while performing your job duties, including:
- Transportation (airfare, train, taxi, mileage)
- Lodging
- Meals during business travel
- Client entertainment
- Office supplies and equipment
- Professional development and training
- Conference and event fees

### When should I submit my expense report?
Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date. Reports submitted after 90 days may not be reimbursed without manager approval.

### What documentation do I need?
- Original itemized receipts for expenses over $25
- Business purpose and attendees for meals/entertainment
- Mileage logs with start/end locations for vehicle expenses
- Conference agendas or registration confirmations

## Travel Expenses

### What is the daily meal allowance?
Per diem rates vary by location:
- Domestic: $75/day ($15 breakfast, $20 lunch, $40 dinner)
- International: Varies by country (consult the travel policy)
- No receipts required for per diem, but you cannot claim both per diem and actual meal expenses

### How do I book travel?
All air and rail travel over $500 must be booked through our approved travel agency. Hotel bookings should aim for the corporate negotiated rate when available.

### Can I use my personal vehicle?
Yes, you can claim mileage at the IRS standard rate (currently $0.67/mile). This covers fuel, maintenance, and insurance. Parking and tolls are reimbursable separately.

## Entertainment & Meals

### What qualifies as business entertainment?
Meals or events with clients, prospects, or business partners where business is discussed. You must document:
- Names and companies of attendees
- Business purpose
- Location and date

### Is alcohol reimbursable?
Alcohol is reimbursable for client entertainment only, up to 2 drinks per person. Personal alcohol consumption is not reimbursable.

### Can I tip on business meals?
Yes, tips up to 20% are reimbursable on business meals and transportation.

## International Travel

### How do I handle foreign currency?
Submit expenses in the original currency. Our system will convert at the exchange rate on the transaction date. Credit card foreign transaction fees are reimbursable.

### Do I need travel insurance?
Company-provided travel insurance is automatic for trips over 3 days. Personal travel insurance is not reimbursable.

## Approval & Processing

### How long does approval take?
- Manager approval: Within 3 business days
- Finance review: Within 5 business days
- Reimbursement: Within 10 business days of final approval via direct deposit

### Why was my expense rejected?
Common rejection reasons:
- Missing or illegible receipts
- Expenses over policy limits without pre-approval
- Personal expenses included
- Incomplete business justification
- Duplicate submissions

### What if I have an urgent reimbursement need?
Contact your finance business partner for expedited processing. This is reserved for hardship cases only.

## Specific Expense Types

### Are home office expenses covered?
Permanent remote employees can claim internet (50%) and phone (50%). Equipment must be pre-approved by IT.

### Can I expense professional memberships?
Yes, if directly related to your role and pre-approved by your manager. This includes professional licenses and certifications.

### What about team events?
Team building activities require VP approval and have a budget of $50 per person per quarter.

## Compliance

### What happens if I violate the policy?
Violations may result in:
- Denied reimbursement
- Required repayment of improper expenses
- Disciplinary action up to termination for intentional fraud

### Can I give a gift to a client?
Client gifts under $100 are allowed with manager approval. Gifts to government officials are strictly prohibited.

### Who can I contact for questions?
- Policy questions: finance@company.com
- Technical issues: helpdesk@company.com
- Manager for expense-specific guidance
